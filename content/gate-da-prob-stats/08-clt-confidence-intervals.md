## Q701

[MCQ]

The Central Limit Theorem states that for a large sample size $n$, the sample mean $\bar{X}$ drawn from a population with mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$ is approximately distributed as:

- **(A)** $N\!\left(\mu,\ \sigma^2\right)$
- **(B)** $N\!\left(\mu,\ \dfrac{\sigma^2}{n}\right)$
- **(C)** $N\!\left(0,\ 1\right)$
- **(D)** $N\!\left(\mu,\ n\sigma^2\right)$

**Answer:** B

## Q705

[MCQ]

A 95% confidence interval for a population mean is computed as $\bar{x} \pm 1.96 \cdot \dfrac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$. If $\bar{x} = 100$, $\sigma = 10$, and $n = 25$, what is the width of the confidence interval?

- **(A)** 3.92
- **(B)** 7.84
- **(C)** 19.6
- **(D)** 1.96

**Answer:** B

## Q706

[NAT]

A 95% CI is $\bar{x} \pm 1.96 \cdot \text{SE}$ where $\text{SE} = \sigma/\sqrt{n}$. With $\sigma = 8$ and $n = 64$, what is the half-width (margin of error) of the 95% CI?

$$\text{SE} = 8/8 = 1, \quad \text{margin} = 1.96 \times 1 = 1.96$$

**Answer:** 1.96

## Q709

[NAT]

To estimate a population mean within a margin of error $E = 5$ at the 95% confidence level ($z = 1.96$), with known $\sigma = 20$, what is the minimum required sample size $n$?

$$n = \left(\frac{z \cdot \sigma}{E}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{1.96 \times 20}{5}\right)^2 = (7.84)^2 = 61.4656 \approx 62$$

**Answer:** 62

---

## Q710

[NAT]

Using the same formula $n = (z\sigma/E)^2$, if $\sigma = 10$, $E = 2$, and $z = 1.96$, what is the minimum sample size (round up to the nearest integer)?

$$n = \left(\frac{1.96 \times 10}{2}\right)^2 = (9.8)^2 = 96.04 \approx 97$$

**Answer:** 97
